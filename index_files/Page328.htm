As she glanced up at one of the few people in line and asked them, "For here or to go," he snapped, "To go. And can you hurry up? I have class in ten minutes and this line has been taking forever. I don't understand what the holdup could be."

The girl's happy bubble popped. She served him his meal as quickly as possible then handed it to him without a word. He walked away and she took a second just to breathe deeply. Yep, this job was just as bad as she thought it would be. Thanks, mom, for quitting your job and forcing me to work, she thought. She sniffed back tears but couldn't wipe her eyes because of the gloves she was wearing.

She tried to look nonchalant as she glanced up at the last girl in line. The girl had a look of sympathy on her face that was almost too much to handle. "What's with that guy?" the girl in line asked. "You're doing great. And seriously, next time you serve him, don't give him much food. He doesn't deserve it."

The girl felt gratitude seep into her. She smiled at the girl in line and made sure to give her an extra scoop of potatoes without her asking. "Have a wonderful day. And thank you," she told the girl.
